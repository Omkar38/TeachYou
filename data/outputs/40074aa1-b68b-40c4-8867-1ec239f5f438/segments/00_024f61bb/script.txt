Okay, let’s tackle a really common problem in computer science: exploring graphs. A graph is simply a collection of points – we call them ‘nodes’ – connected by lines – ‘edges’. Think of it like a map with cities and roads. Now, how do we systematically visit every city on that map? That’s where Depth-First Search, or DFS, comes in.

DFS is an algorithm that explores a graph by going as deep as possible along each branch before backtracking. Imagine you’re exploring a maze – you’d pick a path and keep going until you hit a dead end, then you’d go back and try a different path.  It’s a powerful technique, especially in solving problems on platforms like LeetCode, where graph traversal is a frequent pattern.  We’ll be using this approach to find solutions to many problems.
