Okay, let’s talk about a really smart trick called RAG – Retrieval-Augmented Generation. Large Language Models, or LLMs, like ChatGPT, are amazing at generating text, but they have a huge problem: they’re only as good as the data they were trained on. That data is *massive*, but it’s also a snapshot in time. It can be outdated, and it doesn’t always have the specific information you need. 

So, RAG steps in. It’s like giving the LLM a super-powered research assistant. First, you give it a question. Then, RAG *retrieves* – that means it searches a database of up-to-date information, like a giant collection of documents. Finally, it *augments* – it adds this retrieved information to the original question, allowing the LLM to generate a much more accurate and relevant answer. Think of it as boosting the LLM’s knowledge!
